<Section icon={Swords} title="Core Roles and Unit Synergies">

A balanced team is the backbone of any successful run. In this **roll anime to fight team building guide**, we categorize fighters into three distinct roles: Focused DPS, AoE Coverage, and Speed Utility. Filling your slots with only one type of fighter—even if they are all Mythic—will lead to failure when waves become more diverse.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Focused DPS">
    - **Primary Goal**: Single-target destruction.
    - **Key Stats**: High Damage per hit, Long Range.
    - **Best for**: Bosses and high-health Elite enemies.
  </Card>
  <Card title="AoE Coverage">
    - **Primary Goal**: Clearing mob clusters.
    - **Key Stats**: Wide attack area, moderate CD.
    - **Best for**: Preventing enemies from leaking during swarm waves.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Speed Utility">
    - **Primary Goal**: Constant damage uptime.
    - **Key Stats**: Low CD (Cooldown), fast animations.
    - **Best for**: Finishing off low-health enemies quickly.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

When selecting your five or six active fighters, aim for a 2-2-1 or 3-2-1 distribution. The "3" should represent your strongest damage dealers, the "2" should be your crowd-clearers, and the "1" should be a flexible slot based on your current mutation luck.

| Role Type | Recommended Fighters | Optimal Mutation | Strategy Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focused DPS** | Mythic Swordsmen | **Titan** or **Nen** | Place in central spots to maximize range. |
| **AoE Coverage** | Elemental Casters | **Gold** or **Diamond** | Focus on units with circular or cone attacks. |
| **Speed/Finish** | Martial Artists | **Nen** | Use to catch fast enemies that slip through. |

<Callout type="tip" title="Synergy Bonus">
Look for units that have overlapping attack ranges. If your AoE unit weakens a group, your Speed unit should be able to instantly clean them up without your high-damage units wasting a slow, powerful hit on a sliver of health.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Merging and Leveling System">

The merge system is where your team truly gains the power needed for high-wave checkpoints. Every time you roll a duplicate of a fighter you already own, you have the opportunity to increase the "Main" copy's level. However, the most common mistake beginners make is merging away valuable mutations.

<Callout type="warning" title="Mutation Alert">
Before clicking merge, check the labels! A "Titan" Legendary is a completely different upgrade path than a "Normal" Legendary. Never merge a mutated unit into a normal unit unless you are absolutely sure you don't want the mutation bonus.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Alpha Copy">
    Compare all versions of a specific fighter. Choose the one with the best mutation (Titan > Nen > Diamond > Gold > Normal) to be your primary unit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Lock Your Valuable Units">
    Use the in-game "Lock" feature on your primary mutated fighters to prevent accidental merging or deletion during busy rolling sessions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Accumulate Standard Duplicates">
    Roll for more units using your earned currency. Standard (non-mutated) duplicates should be fed into your "Alpha" copy to raise its combat level.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Merge">
    Open the Merge menu, select your Alpha unit, and add the matching duplicates. You will see the DPS and Range stats increase immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Monitor Stat Scaling">
    As units level up, their CD often remains the same while Damage and Range scale. If a unit's Range becomes massive, consider repositioning them in your team lineup.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| **1 - 10** | Low (Common Duplicates) | +20% Total DPS |
| **11 - 25** | Moderate (Rare/Leg Duplicates) | +50% Total DPS |
| **26 - 50** | High (Matching Rarity) | +100% Total DPS |

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Mutation Tiers and Keep Priorities">

Mutations are the "X-factor" in any **roll anime to fight team building guide**. They provide flat percentage buffs to damage, speed, or range that cannot be obtained through standard leveling alone. Understanding which mutations to keep and which to use as fodder is essential for resource management.

| Mutation | Tier | Stat Bonus (Approx) | Best Role |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Titan** | **S** | +200% Damage | Focused DPS / Boss Killers |
| **Nen** | **S** | +100% Speed / +50% Damage | Speed Utility / AoE |
| **Diamond** | **A** | +75% Damage / +25% Range | All-around Balance |
| **Gold** | **B** | +40% Damage | Mid-game Progression |
| **None** | **C** | Base Stats Only | Merge Material |

<Callout type="success" title="The Mutation Meta">
Currently, the **Titan** mutation is the undisputed king of the meta. Even a Legendary Titan can often out-damage a standard Mythic because the 200% damage multiplier applies to the final calculated stat after merging.
</Callout>

When you roll a mutation, it creates a "branch." For example, if you have a Level 20 Normal unit and you roll a Level 1 Titan version of the same unit, you should start leveling the Titan version from scratch. It will eventually surpass the Level 20 Normal unit in raw power.

</Section>
